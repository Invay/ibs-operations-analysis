# Operations Management Analysis — International Business School (IBS), Budapest

An analytical report applying core operations management tools to a real organization — IBS, a private business university in Budapest — to identify bottlenecks and propose concrete improvements.

## Overview

This project analyzes IBS across its supply chain position, product/service development, facility location, operating system, layout, and quality assurance — then ties findings back to the five operations performance objectives (quality, speed, dependability, flexibility, cost).

## Methods & tools used

- **Supply chain mapping** — identified immediate suppliers (University of Buckingham, faculty/staff, Erasmus partners) and customers
- **Factor rating method** — scored the current campus location against two alternatives on weighted criteria
- **Process mapping** — traced the full curriculum-to-degree pipeline to surface bottlenecks
- **Relationship chart & facility layout evaluation** — assessed adjacency of key zones (classrooms, library, administration)
- **Why-why (root cause) analysis** — broke down the drivers of student attrition
- **Pareto chart** — ranked reasons students leave without completing their degree

## Key findings

- **Location scores best as-is.** Despite testing two alternative sites with stronger public transport links, the factor rating method confirmed the current Graphisoft Park location outperforms them once quiet-study environment and dormitory proximity are weighted in.

  ![Factor rating table](images/factor-rating-table.png)

- **The core bottleneck is single-point dependency.** Curriculum development sits entirely with the partner university, and teaching/grading relies heavily on individual instructors — creating capacity constraints and grading delays.

  ![Process map](images/process-map.png)

- **Layout is close to optimal, with one gap.** A relationship-chart evaluation of the three-building layout found only one real issue: no restroom access near the library floor.

  ![Facility layout sketch](images/facility-layout-sketch.png)
  ![Relationship chart](images/relationship-chart-diagram.png)

- **Attrition traces back to three root causes**: ineffective marketing/high visibility cost, financial pressure or underpreparedness among enrolled students, and a gap between some programs and employer market needs.

  ![Why-why analysis](images/why-why-analysis.png)
  ![Pareto chart](images/pareto-chart.png)

## Recommendations

- Introduce blended learning (pre-recorded core lectures) to reduce instructor bottlenecks
- Add grading assistants (human or AI-supported) to reduce grading delays
- Expand AI/data-focused program offerings given rising market demand
- Increase scholarship availability tied to family income, not just academic merit, to improve accessibility
